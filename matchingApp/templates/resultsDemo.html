Explanation of the FaSt Algorithm

FaSt (Fair and Stable matching) is an efficient algorithm designed to compute a fair and stable matching between students and colleges under ranked isometric valuations.

The key features of this algorithm are:

  • Isometric Valuations: The valuation of a student for a college is the same as the college's valuation for that student, ensuring fairness from both perspectives.
  • Leximin Optimality: FaSt ensures that the worst-off agent (student or college) is as well-off as possible in the final matching, and among those matchings, it further optimizes the second-worst-off, and so on.
  • Stability: The algorithm guarantees that there are no blocking pairs, meaning no student and college would prefer to switch their assignments, maintaining a stable matching.

FaSt systematically finds the leximin optimal matching by iterating over the space of stable matchings, improving fairness at each step while ensuring stability.

Explanation of the FaSt-Gen Algorithm

FaSt-Gen (Generalized Fair and Stable matching) extends the FaSt algorithm to more complex and generalized settings where valuations are not necessarily isometric but still ranked.

The key features of this algorithm are:

  • Generalized Ranked Valuations: FaSt-Gen works in scenarios where students and colleges have rankings, but the valuations (preferences) of students and colleges for each other may differ.
  • Leximin Optimality: Similar to FaSt, this algorithm aims to maximize fairness by ensuring that the worst-off agent is as well-off as possible, and further optimizes the second-worst-off, and so on.
  • Flexibility: Unlike FaSt, which is restricted to isometric valuations, FaSt-Gen can handle general valuations, making it applicable to a broader range of matching problems.
  • Efficiency: The algorithm finds the optimal matching in quadratic time relative to the number of students and colleges, even in these more general settings.

FaSt-Gen is designed to handle more generalized scenarios where fairness and stability are still paramount, ensuring balanced and optimal matchings between students and colleges.
